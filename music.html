MUSIC

Whether it's performing in pit orchestras for school musicals, jamming with others at open mic nights, or playing the piano without an audience, music has always been a major part of my life. It all started in the 4th grade when my grade school music teacher said I should give the trombone a shot, most likely due to the drought of trombonists at my school at the time. Well, it turned out that I had a knack for the brass monster.

I didn't stop at the trombone though. I picked up the alto saxophone in 6th grade and started piano in the 7th. And while I never did much with the saxophone, I flourished when seated behind the black and white keys. My big breakthrough on piano came in the 8th grade when I was allowed to play in the pit orchestra for You're a Good Man, Charlie Brown, despite my inexperience on piano. I practiced for hours on end well past a typical middle-schooler's bedtime, much to the chagrin of my parents' sleep schedules. I ran through the music so many times that I ended up committing the entire score to memory! Later that year, our school's production made a trip up to New York City for a musical festival where I was fortunate enough to perform on a 9-foot Steinway & Sons concert grand piano! I wouldn't play on another such example for another 12 years.

The summer before 10th grade marked a major change for my musical appetite; I was put into a 3-day jazz camp (lol jazz camp) where I worked with professional musicians to learn the ins and outs, or the basics in my case, of jazz improvisation. I had always had an interest in jazz from a young age ever since being introduced to Miles Davis's Kind of Blue and Dave Brubeck's Time Out by my father, but this camp elevated that interest to another level. After that camp, I became enamored with playing jazz—I ended up forming my own jazz combo, auditioning (and placing) into a state jazz ensemble, and arranging and composing big band music for my high school to play.

This musical hobby of mine has done wonderful things for me. From earning a scholarship at Pitt, traveling to Jamaica, performing for church services, and even singing (badly) at weddings, playing music has given a color to my life that I would never have had otherwise.
